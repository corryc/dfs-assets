Issue 1 — Prohibited Terms (Check 4)
What's wrong
Three prohibited terms appear: "independent", "no bias", and "guaranteed". Under s160B of the National Credit Act, a broker who receives commissions from lenders cannot describe their service as independent, impartial, or unbiased. "Guaranteed" approval is never accurate for mortgage products.
RG 234 reference
RG 234.99–108; National Credit Act s160B; RG 234 Example 58
Phrases flagged
Independent Mortgage Broker   We work independently — no bias   guaranteed home loan approval
Suggested fix
Remove "independent", "no bias" entirely. Replace "guaranteed home loan approval" with: "We work hard to match you with the right lender from our panel — eligibility criteria apply."
Issue 2 — Misleading Headline Claims (Check 2)
What's wrong
"100% success rate" is a claim that ASIC has specifically cited as misleading for mortgage brokers (RG 234 Example 58). "No credit check required for pre-approval" is factually wrong — lenders run credit checks. "Australia's #1" is a superlative with no evidential basis stated.
RG 234 reference
RG 234.63–82; Example 58
Phrases flagged
100% success rate   No credit check required   Australia's #1
Suggested fix
Replace with: "We've helped hundreds of Australians find a home loan that works for them — results depend on individual circumstances and lender eligibility criteria." Remove the #1 claim unless you have third-party verified data supporting it.
Issue 3 — Missing Comparison Rate (Check 3)
What's wrong
"Rates from 5.49% p.a." triggers a mandatory comparison rate disclosure under s160 of the National Credit Code. A comparison rate must appear immediately alongside in equal or greater prominence, plus the mandatory warning statement.
RG 234 reference
RG 234.83–98; National Credit Code s160, s163, s164
Suggested fix
Rates from 5.49% p.a. Comparison rate 5.82% p.a.*
*WARNING: This comparison rate is true only for the examples given and may not include all fees and charges. Different terms, fees or other loan amounts might result in a different comparison rate.

Or: remove the rate entirely and say "competitive rates — ask us for a personalised comparison."
Issue 4 — Scope of Service (Check 6)
What's wrong
"Access every lender in Australia" overstates the broker's panel. ASIC's Example 64 directly addresses this scenario.
RG 234 reference
RG 234.109–118; Example 64
Suggested fix
"Compare home loans from our panel of leading Australian lenders"
Issue 5 — ACL Number Absent (Check 5)
What's wrong
This is a paid advertisement. Under s52 of the National Credit Act and reg 13, the ACL number must appear in all printed advertisements — which includes static digital display ads.
Suggested fix
Add to footer of the ad: "Digital Finance Solutions Pty Ltd | Australian Credit Licence [ACL number]"

Nothing to preserve from this ad

  • The call-to-action buttons are fine
  • Mentioning "bad credit" borrowers is not prohibited — but the framing "even with bad credit" needs the same eligibility qualification as other claims
  • The 1,000+ clients claim is salvageable if reframed: "We've helped over 1,000 Australians with their home loan journey — individual results vary"

Issue 1 — Benefit Claim Balance (Check 1)
What's wrong
The paragraph starting "Refinancing at the right time can save you thousands" does qualify itself ("but the right time depends on your individual circumstances…") — and for a blog article this level of balance is generally acceptable. However, ASIC's guidance is that each component is assessed independently. The second sentence of that paragraph does the qualifying work that the first sentence lacks. As written this is a borderline FLAG rather than a FAIL.
RG 234 reference
RG 234.42–62
Suggested fix
Merge the qualifying clause into the benefit sentence: "Refinancing at the right time — depending on your circumstances and the fees involved — could save you thousands over the life of your loan."

What's working well

  • Lender panel correctly qualified as "a panel of Australia's leading lenders" — not "every lender"
  • No prohibited terms anywhere in the copy
  • Disclaimer is present, clear, and appropriately positioned for editorial content
  • Tone is informative, not promotional-aggressive — fits ASIC's guidance on educational content
  • Mentioning comparison rates in the article outline (last paragraph) is proactively compliant
