House and Land Packages Melbourne: A First Home Buyer's Guide

Understand how house and land packages work, what costs are involved, and how to evaluate whether they represent good value for your situation.

House and land packages have become popular among first home buyers in Melbourne, offering the appeal of a brand new home with clarity around price and quality. But they come with hidden costs and trade-offs worth understanding before committing.

What Is a House and Land Package?

A house and land package bundles a parcel of land with a new home built by a developer. You purchase the complete package at a fixed price, typically with some level of choice in finishes, layouts, and inclusions.

The developer handles the construction and often absorbs some of the administrative burden, which can appeal to first-time buyers. However, the convenience comes at a cost—package pricing may include developer margins that aren't reflected in the broader market.

Always ask for a detailed breakdown of what's included in the package price. Many add-ons like land preparation, foundation work, or utility connections may be optional extras.

Key Costs to Watch For

Package prices often hide secondary costs that first-time buyers overlook:

  • Stamp duty on both land and building
  • Site preparation, drainage, and foundation work
  • Utility connections (water, gas, electricity)
  • Building insurance during construction
  • Council fees and planning approvals

Request an itemised list of what's included and excluded, and factor additional costs into your budget before committing.

Are House and Land Packages Good Value?

Whether a package represents good value depends on the specific location, the developer, and your circumstances. First home buyers sometimes pay a premium for convenience and transparent pricing, while investors comparing packages against established property may find better ROI elsewhere.

Compare Before Committing

Do your research. Compare the package price and features against similar new builds and established properties in the same suburb. A lower price per square metre may indicate good value or weak market positioning—understand which before you buy.

Advantages
  • New home with building warranty
  • Fixed price and completion date clarity
  • No surprise renovations needed immediately
Considerations
  • May pay a developer margin above market value
  • Limited ability to negotiate price
  • Hidden or additional costs common

Location and Depreciation Risk

House and land packages often target emerging suburbs on Melbourne's fringe where land is cheaper. While these areas may appreciate over time, they can also experience slower growth than established suburbs closer to the CBD.

Consider commute times, local amenities, and growth forecasts before choosing a package in a developing area. New builds in established suburbs may hold value better long-term, though they typically carry higher prices.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a house and land package?↓

A house and land package combines a parcel of land with a new home built by a developer. The package price typically includes both the land and construction, often with upgraded fittings and finishes included in the deal.

Are house and land packages a good investment?↓

House and land packages can work well for first home buyers seeking new construction and certainty. However, they may carry higher margins than buying established property. Compare package pricing against local market values before committing.

What costs are included in a house and land package?↓

Packages typically include the land and building construction, but may exclude stamp duty, council rates, connection fees for utilities, and site works. Always ask the developer for a detailed breakdown of what's included and excluded.
