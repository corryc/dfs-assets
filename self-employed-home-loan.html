Self-Employed Home Loans: Your Complete Approval Guide

Learn how to navigate the home loan process as a self-employed borrower, what lenders need, and how to strengthen your application for approval.

Self-employed borrowers face different lending requirements than salaried employees, but home loans are absolutely achievable with the right preparation and guidance.

Can Self-Employed People Get Home Loans?

Yes. However, lenders need to verify your income differently than they do for employees. Instead of a payslip and tax file number match, they'll review your tax returns, business financials, and bank statements to assess your income stability.

Self-employed lending is common. Many lenders specialize in assessing self-employed borrowers and have streamlined processes for income verification.

Trading History Requirements

Most mainstream lenders prefer 2+ years of trading history. However, some lenders work with newer businesses, particularly if you have relevant prior employment in the same field, personal assets, or a larger deposit.

  • 2 years: Standard requirement; easiest approval path
  • 1 year: Some lenders approve with larger deposit or prior industry experience
  • Less than 1 year: Rare but possible with strong personal credit and significant equity

Income Documentation Required

Lenders will assess your income using multiple documents to establish a clear, consistent earnings history:

  • 2 years of personal tax returns
  • 2 years of business tax returns or financial statements
  • Accountant-prepared profit & loss statement
  • 3-6 months of business and personal bank statements
  • Australian Business Register (ABR) extract
  • Any relevant contracts or client agreements
Get Organized Early

Start collecting these documents before applying. Organized documentation speeds up assessment and reduces the chance of your application being declined for missing information.

Strategies to Strengthen Your Application

Self-employed borrowers can take several steps to improve their approval chances:

Boost Approval Chances
  • Maximize your deposit (20%+ strongly favored)
  • Show income growth over 2+ years
  • Maintain excellent credit history
  • Have accountant prepare formal statements
Common Obstacles
  • Inconsistent or declining income
  • Large expenses reducing net profit
  • Gaps in business operation
  • Poor personal credit history

Frequently Asked Questions

Can self-employed people get home loans?↓

Yes, but the process is more involved than for employed borrowers. Lenders need to verify your income using tax returns, accountant statements, and bank records rather than a simple payslip. Many lenders specialize in self-employed lending.

How long do you need to be self-employed?↓

Most lenders prefer 2+ years of trading history. However, some lenders work with businesses trading for 1 year or less, depending on your industry and track record. Shorter trading history may affect rates or deposit requirements.

What income documentation do self-employed borrowers need?↓

Typically: 2 years of tax returns, accountant-prepared financial statements, business activity statements, recent bank statements (3-6 months), and proof of business registration. Some lenders accept alternative documentation depending on your situation.
