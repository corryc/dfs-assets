How I Repaired My Equifax Credit Report

If you are reading this, chances are you've had, have, or are having issues with your credit report. Recently I purchased an investment property. The lender I was using, whom I've worked with many times in the past, had an issue with my credit report which prevented me from obtaining finance.

There was a misspelling of my surname on my Equifax report. This meant my lender would not approve my finance without this credit report being amended to reflect the correct spelling of my name as per my government ID.

Given I had already paid a substantial deposit and settlement was only 60 days away, this relatively small issue (one incorrect letter) on my credit report had the potential to cost me tens of thousands of dollars if I were to lose my deposit.

Two Options to Consider

Option 1: Engage a credit repair company. This can be a costly process. If you're reading this blog, it's likely you've already researched some of these companies. They can achieve results but there is no guarantee of a successful outcome. Credit repair companies can cost upwards of $1,500.

Option 2: Amend your report yourself. This is what I ultimately decided to do. The only thing it cost was my time to lodge the dispute and, of course, the 30 days I had to wait for a response.

This is a step-by-step guide for how I went about amending my own report. It is just a guide and there's no guarantee of a positive result, even if you follow the same steps. The decision ultimately rests with Equifax if they amend your report.

Step-by-Step Guide to Fixing Your Equifax Report

Step 1: Research Before You Start

There are many credit repair companies online. Before engaging any of these companies, it's important to do your research. Understand the costs involved and whether a company is right for your situation.

Step 2: Visit the Equifax Corrections Portal Online

Firstly, you need to visit the Equifax Corrections Portal online. This is where you can begin your process. In my case, I clicked through to Credit Report Corrections.

Step 3: Select Your Report Type

In the corrections portal, I selected Individual Credit Report, as it was my own individual credit report I was going to be contesting. There is also an option for Commercial Credit Data if you're disputing a commercial credit report.

Then select "Individual Directly" as you are completing this yourself.

Step 4: Accept Terms and Conditions

Read and review the terms and conditions. If you agree, click "Yes". Ideally, you should already have a copy of your credit report so you know what you're contesting. It would be difficult to prepare a case for contesting misinformation if you don't have your credit report to confirm exactly what it is.

Step 5: Input Your Personal Information

It's important to enter your personal information correctly. As a guide, if you have a passport, use what is on your passport. Enter the following information as accurately as possible.

In some instances, Equifax may have employment history information which will need to match up with what is on their system.

Step 6: Identify and Select the Correction Type

Because I was contesting a matter on my personal report, I selected Consumer Credit Data. There is also an option for Commercial Credit Data on the same dropdown.

In the second dropdown, you can see all of the potential issues you can contest through the Equifax portal:

  • Enquiry
  • Default
  • Account Information
  • Identity Information
  • Repayment History
  • Court Writ / Default Judgement
  • Financial Hardship Information
  • Bankruptcy Correction
  • Default Correction Due to Bankruptcy
  • Directorship / Proprietorship

While you can technically contest all of the above items, there is no guarantee of a successful outcome. For example, with a default, you will also be required to contact the credit provider that initially listed the default.

Step 7: Provide Correction Details

In this section, you'll get more specific about what information you wish to contest. I'd recommend keeping your response professional, polite, and factual.

I knew the information was wrong and had multiple IDs confirming the correct spelling, so I was able to present a strong case. I also stated the urgency and time frames involved.

Step 8: State Incorrect Details

Accurately state the incorrect information you're looking to correct on your credit report.

Step 9: Upload Supporting Evidence

This step is critical. Have scans of your supporting documents to upload. For example, with a name misspelling error, uploading multiple government IDs with the correct spelling will give you the best chance of success.

Step 10: Submit Additional Requests if Required

Once you've submitted your request, there will be an option to submit additional requests if there are other matters you wish to amend on your credit file.

After Your Correction Request Is Submitted

You will receive a confirmation email from Equifax where you'll be supplied a tracking number and a link to the online portal where you can check the progress of your request.

Equifax has a 30-day response time, which is required by law. However, you may receive a second email requesting an extension.

The final email you'll receive from Equifax is the response email, which attaches a password-protected PDF with the outcome of your request.

In my case, my initial request was lodged on 6 August, and the response email came through on 4 September, 29 days in total.
