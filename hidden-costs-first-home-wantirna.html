The Hidden Costs of Buying Your First Home

What Wantirna first home buyers actually spend beyond the deposit, from stamp duty to moving day.

Buying your first home in Wantirna feels exciting, but many costs sit under the surface of the advertised price. This guide runs through the main hidden costs that first home buyers in Victoria face so you can plan ahead with clearer numbers.

Victorian stamp duty and first home buyer concessions

For many first home buyers in Wantirna, the largest hidden cost is Victorian land transfer duty, commonly called stamp duty. Stamp duty is a state government tax on property transactions. In Victoria, the amount you may pay depends on the price of the property, whether you will live in it as your principal place of residence, and whether you qualify as a first home buyer under current rules. As at 2026, State Revenue Office Victoria sets a sliding scale of duty rates that generally increase with the dutiable value of the property. For home buyers who do not qualify for a concession, duty on a typical Wantirna house price can run into tens of thousands of dollars, so it is important to obtain an estimate early using the State Revenue Office Victoria calculator or through your conveyancer. Victorian first home buyer exemptions and concessions may reduce or remove this cost if you meet certain conditions. According to State Revenue Office Victoria, eligible first home buyers who purchase a principal place of residence valued up to a threshold such as 600,000 dollars may receive a full exemption from stamp duty, while purchases above that and up to a higher cap, often around 750,000 dollars, may qualify for a duty concession rather than a full exemption. These thresholds, residency requirements, and what counts as a first home buyer can change, so it's worth confirming current figures on the State Revenue Office Victoria website before signing a contract.

Hidden costs can add tens of thousands to the real price of buying in Wantirna, so it pays to know what might be coming before you sign a contract.

Lenders mortgage insurance and the 20% deposit threshold

One cost that catches many first home buyers off guard is lenders mortgage insurance (LMI). LMI is a one-off insurance premium that you may be charged if you borrow more than a certain proportion of the property value - most commonly when your deposit is under 20%. While it protects the lender, not you, it is usually paid by the borrower.

  • Check if you qualify for any Victorian first home buyer stamp duty exemptions or concessions and confirm current thresholds with State Revenue Office Victoria.
  • Ask your conveyancer or solicitor for a full written quote covering professional fees, search fees, and disbursements before you engage them.
  • Request an LMI estimate from your broker or lender upfront if your deposit will be under 20% of the property price.
  • Budget for building and pest inspections on every property you are seriously considering, not just the one you end up buying, as you may miss out on some offers or auctions along the way.

If your deposit is less than 20% of the property price, it is worth discussing LMI and alternatives before you make an offer. A mortgage broker can help you understand whether a higher deposit, a different loan structure, splitting your loan, or a government support scheme might suit your situation and reduce or remove LMI. They can also request LMI estimates from lenders so you can include them in your total cost calculations.

Conveyancing and legal fees in Victoria

Legal work for a Victorian property purchase is another area where hidden costs can creep in. For a standard residential purchase in suburbs like Wantirna, many buyers engage either a conveyancer or a solicitor to handle the contract review, section 32 vendor statement, property and title searches, and the settlement process. Typical professional fees for conveyancing in Victoria can range from several hundred dollars to a few thousand dollars, depending on the complexity of the transaction, whether you need additional contract advice, and whether you are buying at auction or by private sale. These figures are based on typical market ranges reported by Victorian consumer and legal information sites, and are subject to change. On top of the professional fee, you can expect disbursements - costs that your conveyancer pays on your behalf - for items such as title searches, rates and water authority certificates, and other regulatory checks. These may add a few hundred dollars or more. Because each property and local council has different search requirements, the final amount varies. To avoid surprises, ask for a written quote that separates: - the fixed professional fee (if any) - estimated search fees and disbursements - any extra charges for urgent work, contract changes, or attending multiple auctions. You may also want your conveyancer or solicitor to review the contract and section 32 before you bid at auction or make an unconditional offer. Some firms charge separately for pre-auction contract reviews, while others include them in a package. Clarifying this early helps you budget for the entire buying journey, not just the property that you eventually secure.

Other hidden costs: inspections, loan fees, and moving

Beyond stamp duty, LMI, and conveyancing, a number of smaller but still significant costs often arise when buying your first home in Wantirna. Each one may seem manageable on its own, but together they can make a real difference to your savings and borrowing capacity. Key extra costs to consider include building and pest inspections, loan application and valuation fees, and the practical expenses of moving house. Building and pest inspections A professional building inspection in Victoria is strongly recommended for freestanding houses and townhouses, especially in established suburbs like Wantirna where properties may be several decades old. Typical inspection fees can range from a few hundred dollars upward, depending on the size, type, and complexity of the property and whether you combine building and pest checks into one visit. These ranges are based on quotes commonly reported by Victorian building inspection services and may change over time. If you are inspecting multiple properties - for example, before bidding at several auctions - you may pay for several inspections without successfully purchasing each home. It is sensible to allow a buffer in your budget for this. Loan application and valuation fees When you apply for a home loan, the lender may charge application, documentation, or settlement fees. Some loans also involve a separate valuation fee for the property, where the lender arranges a professional valuer to confirm its market value. In some cases, these fees may be waived or built into the overall loan cost, but this depends on the lender and specific product. As a rough guide, typical application and valuation fees in the Victorian market can range from a few hundred dollars combined to higher amounts for more complex lending, according to public lender fee schedules available online. Because fee structures can vary widely and change over time, it is important to obtain up-to-date figures from your broker or directly from the lender before applying. Moving costs The practical side of moving also carries real costs. Depending on your situation, you may need to budget for: - professional removalists - packing materials, boxes, and protective wrapping - short-term storage - end-of-lease cleaning if you are moving from a rental - connection fees for electricity, gas, internet, and water at your new home. Removalist quotes in Melbourne often depend on the number of bedrooms, amount of furniture, access and stair issues, and whether you move on a weekend or weekday. Typical moves within metro Melbourne, including Wantirna, can cost from several hundred dollars upward, based on price guides published by Victorian removalist and consumer advice sites. Connection fees for utilities vary by provider and plan. Some services may charge an upfront connection or transfer fee, while others may not. It is helpful to contact your chosen providers in advance to confirm costs and timing. While none of these items match stamp duty or LMI in size, together they can easily add a few thousand dollars to your overall budget.

Pros
  • Fewer surprises: Understanding the hidden costs means you are less likely to be caught off guard at settlement or when bills start arriving.
  • Better budgeting: Factoring in stamp duty, LMI, conveyancing, inspections, and moving upfront helps you set a more realistic savings goal and borrowing limit.
  • Stronger negotiation: When you know your total purchase cost, not just the advertised price, you can negotiate with more confidence on both the property and your loan structure.
Cons
  • Extra planning time: Working through all the hidden costs and getting quotes from different professionals can take time and energy on top of an already stressful process.
  • Upfront cash needed: Some costs such as building inspections, legal fees, and moving expenses need to be paid in cash and cannot usually be rolled into your home loan.
  • Complexity: Rules around stamp duty concessions, LMI, and government schemes can be complex and may change, so it can feel hard to keep track of what applies to you.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much stamp duty do first home buyers pay in Victoria?↓

In Victoria, first home buyers may receive a full stamp duty exemption on a principal place of residence valued up to a threshold such as 600,000 dollars, and a concession on homes valued above that and up to a higher cap, according to State Revenue Office Victoria. Thresholds, rules, and any special Wantirna or metro area considerations can change, so it is important to check current figures on the State Revenue Office Victoria website or with your conveyancer before you sign a contract.

Can I pay most of these costs at settlement instead of upfront?↓

In many cases you can, but it depends on the contract and the provider. Some conveyancers and solicitors may allow payment of their professional fees at settlement, but search fees, disbursements, and some components often need to be paid earlier. Removalists and cleaners usually expect payment on or shortly after moving day. It is worth asking each provider about their payment terms upfront.

Is lenders mortgage insurance an ongoing monthly fee?↓

No, LMI is not a monthly fee in the same way as council rates or utilities. It is usually a one-off premium that may be added to your home loan or paid upfront when you borrow more than a certain percentage of the property value. The exact structure and cost depends on the lender and insurer, and on your loan size and deposit.
