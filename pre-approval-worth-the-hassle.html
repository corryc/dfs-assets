What is Home Loan Pre-Approval?

A home loan pre-approval is a preliminary assessment where a lender evaluates your financial position and indicates how much they may be willing to lend. It's a conditional statement based on your income, credit history, existing debts, and savings.

Pre-approval is not a guarantee. It signals that the lender has reviewed your finances and is interested in lending to you, subject to satisfactory property valuation and legal checks.

Think of it as a "green light" before you start house hunting, the lender has confirmed you're capable of borrowing a certain amount, so you know your budget and can make offers with confidence.

Why Pre-Approval Matters in Today's Market

In competitive property markets, pre-approval strengthens your negotiating position:

  • Sellers take you seriously. A pre-approval letter shows you're a committed, finance-ready buyer, not a tire kicker.
  • You move faster. Once you find a property you like, you can make an unconditional offer quickly (subject only to valuation and legal review), not conditional on financing.
  • You avoid disappointment. Pre-approval clarifies your borrowing capacity upfront, so you don't fall in love with a property you can't afford.
  • You can compare rates. Pre-approval from multiple lenders lets you see which offers the best rate and terms before committing.

What Do Lenders Check for Pre-Approval?

Lenders typically assess:

  • Income & Employment. How much you earn, job stability, and contract type (permanent, fixed-term, self-employed).
  • Credit History. Payment history, existing debts, defaults, or bankruptcies.
  • Savings & Assets. Deposit amount, savings buffer, and other assets.
  • Existing Debts. Car loans, credit cards, personal loans, anything that affects your serviceability.
  • Expenses. Living costs, family commitments, and other regular outgoings.

On this basis, the lender calculates how much they're willing to lend. This figure is specific to their lending criteria, different lenders may approve different amounts for the same person.

How Long Does Pre-Approval Take?

Pre-approval can typically be completed in 1-5 business days, depending on how quickly you supply documents and how straightforward your circumstances are.

Tips to speed it up:

  • Have your payslips, tax returns, and bank statements ready before applying.
  • Be honest and thorough when answering the lender's questions.
  • Respond promptly to any follow-up requests from the lender or broker.
General information: This article provides general guidance only and does not constitute financial advice. Everyone's circumstances differ. A mortgage broker or financial adviser can provide tailored advice based on your specific situation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does pre-approval guarantee a loan? ▼
No. Pre-approval is conditional on property valuation, satisfactory legal review, and no material change in your financial circumstances. Final approval still depends on these factors.
How long does pre-approval last? ▼
Typically 3-6 months. After that, you may need a fresh assessment, especially if circumstances have changed.
Does it hurt my credit score? ▼
A pre-approval inquiry counts as a credit inquiry and may have a small, temporary impact. Multiple inquiries in a short window (2 weeks) minimise this effect.
Can I get pre-approved from multiple lenders? ▼
Yes. Comparing pre-approval offers from different lenders helps you find the best rate and terms. Group applications within 2 weeks to reduce credit score impact.
