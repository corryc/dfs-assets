What does it actually cost to buy in Ringwood?

Ringwood sits at the intersection of accessibility and liveability — it's on the Belgrave and Lilydale lines, has a proper town centre, and is surrounded by eastern suburbs that have pushed buyers out on price. Demand here has stayed steady even as broader Melbourne values softened.

If you're targeting a house around the $820,000 median, you need roughly $210,000–$215,000 in total funds to cover your deposit, stamp duty, and purchase costs. Stamp duty is the big variable — use the figures below as a rough guide, then get an exact number from the SRO Victoria calculator and confirm with your conveyancer.

How much can you borrow?

Borrowing capacity depends on your income, expenses, existing debts, and number of dependants. As a rough illustration: a couple with combined income of $130,000, no dependants, and no existing debts can typically borrow $700,000–$780,000 depending on the lender. A single income of $100,000 generally sits closer to $500,000–$560,000. These are illustrations only — your actual figure depends on your full financial picture.

Frequently asked questions

Can I buy in Ringwood with a 5% deposit?

Possibly — through the First Home Guarantee scheme, eligible first home buyers can purchase with as little as 5% without paying LMI. Places are limited and eligibility criteria apply. A broker can check whether you qualify.

What is stamp duty on an $820,000 property in Victoria?

For an owner-occupier buying an established home around $820,000, stamp duty is roughly $40,000–$45,000 — but the exact figure depends on your purchase price, property type, and any concessions. Use the SRO Victoria calculator and confirm with your conveyancer before settlement.

How long does pre-approval take?

Most lenders issue conditional pre-approvals within 2–5 business days once all documents are submitted. Having payslips, tax returns, bank statements, and ID ready speeds the process.

Is Ringwood a good area to buy in 2026?

Ringwood offers strong infrastructure, train access, a proper town centre, and relatively affordable prices compared to the inner east. Property decisions should be based on your own research and circumstances — a broker speaks to finance options, not property selection.
