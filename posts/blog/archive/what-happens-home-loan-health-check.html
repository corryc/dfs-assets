Most borrowers take out a home loan and then don't look at it again for years. Lenders count on this. A home loan health check is a structured review of your current rate, loan structure, and features — compared against what the market offers today. It takes about 15 minutes and costs nothing.

What we review in a health check

A proper health check isn't just about the interest rate. We look at six areas that together determine whether your loan is working efficiently.

01

Interest rate

We compare your current rate against equivalent products across 30+ lenders. A gap of more than 0.5% on a $600K loan is typically worth acting on.

02

Loan structure

Is your loan split correctly between fixed and variable? Is interest-only still suitable, or has your situation changed? The right structure depends on your current goals.

03

Offset account usage

If you have an offset, is it linked correctly and are you using it effectively? Many borrowers have an offset they're not maximising — or paying fees for one they don't need.

04

Fees and charges

Annual package fees, monthly account fees, and discharge fees all add to the cost of your loan. We check whether the features justify the fees.

05

Redraw facility

Does your loan have a redraw? Are there restrictions or fees on accessing it? This matters if you've made extra repayments and may want access to them later.

06

Repayment type

Principal and interest versus interest-only affects your equity position and tax implications on investment properties. We check whether your current repayment type still fits your strategy.

The 5-step health check process

1

You share your current loan details

Your current rate, lender, loan balance, and any key features you're using. A recent statement or your loan app is usually enough. This takes around five minutes.

2

We check the market

We run your situation across 30+ lenders and identify products that match or improve on your current loan — rate, structure, and features. This is a genuine comparison, not a shortlist of two.

3

We present the findings

We show you what we found: whether your current loan is competitive, which alternatives are available, and a rough estimate of any saving over your remaining loan term.

4

You decide whether to act

There's no obligation. If your loan is competitive, we'll tell you. If it isn't, we explain what switching would involve — costs, timing, and paperwork. You decide from there.

5

We handle the refinance (if applicable)

If you choose to switch, we manage the full process — application, lender liaison, and settlement. You'll typically be on the new loan within four to six weeks.

What does refinancing actually cost?

Cost is one of the most common reasons people delay switching. The actual figures are often lower than borrowers expect.

Typical refinancing costs

Basic variable loan refinance $800 – $1,000
Fixed rate loan (during fixed period) Significantly higher — break costs apply
Government fees (discharge, transfer) $300 – $600 depending on state
New lender application fee $0 – $600 (many waive this)
Break cost estimates for fixed rate loans vary significantly depending on how much rates have moved and the remaining fixed term. Always confirm the exact figure with your current lender before deciding to switch.

If you're in a fixed rate period, get a break cost figure from your lender before doing anything. Break costs can make switching uneconomical even when there's a significant rate difference. We can help you run the numbers.

For most variable rate loans, the total switching cost sits between $800 and $1,000 for a straightforward refinance. If you're saving $300+ per month on a larger loan, that cost is recovered within a few months.

When should you get a health check?

The most common triggers are:

Your fixed rate is about to expire. This is the single best time to review — when your loan reverts to a variable rate, you often end up on a higher standard variable rate while better deals are available elsewhere.

You haven't reviewed in over 12 months. Lender rate policies shift constantly. A loan that was competitive 18 months ago may no longer be.

Your property has increased in value. A higher property value means a lower LVR — which can unlock better rate tiers at your current lender or open up more competitive options elsewhere.

Your income or expenses have changed significantly. A pay rise, new debt, or a change in employment type can affect what you qualify for and what loan structure makes sense.

Frequently asked questions

How long does a health check take?
The initial review is typically a 15-minute call or email exchange. We gather your current loan details and do the comparison work on our end. You'll have findings within one business day.
Will a health check affect my credit score?
No. A health check is a review of your current loan and a market comparison — no credit enquiry is made. Credit enquiries only happen if you decide to proceed with a refinance application.
What if my loan is competitive?
We'll tell you. If your rate and structure are good, there's no recommendation to switch. We'll note when to check again — typically before your next fixed rate expiry or in 12 months.
Can I get a review if I'm self-employed?
Yes. Self-employed borrowers often benefit most from a review because some lenders are significantly better at assessing variable income than others. We compare across lenders who handle self-employed applications correctly.
I'm on a fixed rate — is it worth reviewing now?
Yes, for two reasons. First, knowing your options means you're ready to act the moment your fixed period ends. Second, if your fixed rate is expiring within the next three months, we can have a new loan ready to settle right as your fixed period ends — with no gap on the higher revert rate.
Do you charge for a health check?
No. The review is free. If you choose to refinance, our fee is paid by the new lender at settlement — not by you. Any fee applicable to your specific situation is disclosed in full before you proceed.
