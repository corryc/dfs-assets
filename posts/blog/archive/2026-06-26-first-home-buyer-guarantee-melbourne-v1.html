The Scheme Explained

How the 5% Deposit Scheme Works

The Australian Government guarantees part of your loan — which may allow you to borrow with a smaller deposit, subject to lender approval and eligibility criteria.

1
Save a 5% Deposit

Instead of needing a 20% deposit to avoid LMI, eligible buyers may be able to purchase with as little as 5% — with the government guaranteeing the remaining portion of the loan.

2
Apply Through a Participating Lender

The scheme is only available through approved participating lenders and authorised mortgage brokers. You can't apply directly through the government — a broker can help you identify which lender suits your situation.

3
Buy Within the Price Cap

Property price caps apply depending on where you're buying. In Melbourne, the current cap is $950,000 for capital city and regional centre purchases. Regional Victoria has a separate cap — speak with a broker to confirm current limits.

Who Qualifies

Eligibility Criteria

The scheme has specific requirements. A broker can confirm whether you meet them and help you identify the right lender.

  • Australian citizens or permanent residents
  • First home buyers (never previously owned property in Australia)
  • Intend to live in the property as your principal place of residence
  • Minimum 5% genuine savings as a deposit
  • Property purchase price within the applicable regional cap
  • Income caps were removed from 1 October 2025 — no income limit applies under the current scheme rules

Eligibility is assessed by the lender, not the government. Meeting the scheme criteria does not guarantee loan approval. Speak with a broker to understand your specific position.
