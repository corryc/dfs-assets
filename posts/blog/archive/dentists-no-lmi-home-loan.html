What dentists typically save on a $900K purchase

LMI is a one-off insurance premium that protects the lender — not you — when borrowing above 80% LVR. On a $900K purchase with a 10% deposit, LMI can exceed $20,000. The dentist no-LMI policy eliminates it entirely.

How does the no-LMI policy work?

When you borrow above 80% of a property's value, lenders typically require LMI to cover their risk if you default. For most borrowers it's a condition of the loan — a premium you pay upfront or capitalise into the loan.

For eligible dental professionals, select lenders waive this requirement. They've assessed that dentists are statistically low-risk borrowers and built that into their lending policy. The result: you can access up to 90–95% LVR without the additional cost.

90% vs 95% LVR: Most dentist no-LMI policies go to 90% LVR (10% deposit), with select lenders extending to 95% LVR (5% deposit). Eligibility criteria are typically stricter at 95%. We can confirm exactly what's available for your situation.

Other things that affect your application

Meeting the professional criteria is the first step. Your application also needs to stack up on standard lending criteria:

Income type matters

Whether you're employed, a contractor, or running your own practice changes how lenders assess your income. A salaried dentist at a hospital or practice is generally straightforward. Practice owners and contractors may need two years of tax returns, and some lenders add back depreciation and other expenses when calculating your borrowing power.

HECS debt reduces borrowing capacity

Dental degrees carry significant HECS debt. Lenders include HECS repayments in your expense assessment, which reduces borrowing capacity. The size of the impact depends on your income — at higher income levels, HECS repayments are higher as a percentage. We factor this in when assessing which lenders give you the best outcome.

Deposit source

The 10% deposit generally needs to be in genuine savings held for at least three months. Gifts from parents may be acceptable with some lenders — confirm before you rely on this.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to be in a permanent role to qualify?
Not necessarily. Dentists working as contractors, locums, or practice owners can qualify — the income assessment just differs. Contractor dentists typically need a 12-month employment history with the same practice or organisation. We'll confirm what applies to your situation.
Can I use the no-LMI policy for an investment property?
Most no-LMI professional loan policies are restricted to owner-occupied purchases. Some lenders extend them to investment properties, but fewer do. We can check which lenders currently allow this.
What documents do I need?
The standard list includes: AHPRA registration certificate, two most recent payslips (or tax returns if self-employed), last two years' group certificates or tax assessments, bank statements showing your deposit, photo ID, and the property contract when available.
Is the interest rate higher with a no-LMI loan?
No. The no-LMI policy is a waiver of the mortgage insurance requirement — it doesn't add a rate premium. You're accessing the lender's standard rate for that product. In some cases, professional loan products are competitively priced against the standard range.
Does this work with the First Home Owner Grant?
Yes, they can be used together. The no-LMI professional loan and the First Home Owner Grant are separate policies — one relates to how lenders assess your application, the other is a government grant. Eligibility for each is assessed independently.
I'm finishing my dental degree soon — can I apply?
Most lenders require active AHPRA registration at the time of application. If you've received provisional registration in your final or intern year, some lenders will accept this. Confirm the timing with us before you start looking at properties.
