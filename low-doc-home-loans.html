Self-employed borrowers often face stricter scrutiny from traditional lenders. Low doc home loans exist to bridge this gap, using alternative assessment methods to evaluate your capacity to service a loan.

What Are Low Doc Home Loans?

Low doc loans allow lenders to assess self-employed borrowers using alternative income verification. Instead of two years of tax returns and PAYG payment summaries, you may provide accountant references, profit and loss statements, bank statements, or even a statutory declaration of income.

Low doc doesn't mean no documentation. Lenders still verify your income; they simply use different methods than traditional employment-based verification.

Documentation Requirements

  • Accountant reference letter or prepared financial statements
  • Business tax returns (1-2 years depending on lender)
  • Bank statements showing regular income deposits (typically 2-3 months)
  • Profit and loss statement or income ledger
  • Business registration documents and any relevant contracts

Requirements vary by lender, so it's worth exploring options with a mortgage broker who specializes in self-employed lending.

Busting the Two-Year Myth

The "you need two years of tax returns" rule is commonly repeated but not universal. Some lenders accept one year of trading history, and newer businesses may be assessed using alternative methods. Your loan amount, deposit size, and business type also affect whether lenders flex this requirement.

Shop Around

Traditional banks often apply stricter rules, while specialist lenders and brokers can navigate alternative assessment methods. Don't assume your application will be rejected without exploring specialist options first.

Interest Rates and Costs

Low doc loans may carry slightly higher interest rates than standard loans, typically 0.1% to 0.5% more, reflecting the additional assessment effort. However, rates vary widely between lenders. Some specialist lenders price competitively despite using alternative verification methods.

Advantages
  • Access to borrowing when traditional banks won't approve
  • Faster assessment using available documentation
  • Competitive rates from specialist lenders
Considerations
  • May pay higher interest rates
  • Accountant fees for reference letters
  • Strict serviceability calculations often applied

Frequently Asked Questions

What are low doc home loans?↓

Low doc loans are designed for self-employed borrowers or those with non-standard income who may struggle to provide traditional employment verification. Lenders assess ability to service using alternative documentation, such as accountant references, business tax returns, or bank statements.

Is the two-year tax return requirement real?↓

Many lenders prefer two years of tax returns to establish income stability. However, some lenders accept one year, and newer businesses may be assessed using other methods. The requirement depends on the lender, your loan amount, and other factors like deposit size.

Will I pay more for a low doc loan?↓

Low doc loans may carry slightly higher interest rates than standard loans, reflecting the additional risk assessment required. However, rates vary significantly between lenders. It's worth comparing rates from specialist lenders who regularly work with self-employed borrowers.
